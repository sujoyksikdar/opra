Binghamton University School of Computing Fall 2025 Course Assignment.

More Information

How to assign a limited number of seats in popular courses to students who have complex preferences over being assigned combinations of courses?

This web application uses student preferences expressed as partial rankings over the courses offered by the School of Computing to decide the assignment of courses to students. The objectives are two-fold: (1) Efficiency, i.e. maximize the total satisfaction of students, which at a high level, is achieved by assigning seats to students that value them the most, and (2) Fairness, i.e. ensure that students with similar preferences are treated similarly.

There are several nuances to this problem. For example: (1) There are many other natural notions of efficiency and fairness to consider. (2) While partial rankings over courses makes it easier to express preferences, it also introduces uncertainties and distortions: How to extend the submitted partial rankings over individual courses to rankings over combinations of courses? (3) What about relative intensities of preferences over the courses? And so on. Resolving these issues is an area of active ongoing research in economics, and also computer science, since these often lead to problems that are computationally hard.

If you would like to learn more, please contact Sujoy Sikdar, who is always happy to discuss resource allocation, matching, computational social science and game theory.
